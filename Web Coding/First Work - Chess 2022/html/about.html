About

Chess is one of the oldest and most popular board games. It is played by two opponents on a checkered board with specially designed pieces of contrasting colours, commonly white and black. The objective of the game is to capture the opponent's king.

Feature

Chess is played on a board of 64 squares arranged in eight vertical rows - files and eight horizontal rows - ranks.
These squares alternate between two colours: one light and the other dark.
The board is set between the two opponents so that each player has a light square at the right-hand corner.

Objective

When a player moves a piece to a square on which it attacks the enemy king that is, a square from which it could capture the king if the king is not shielded or movedâ€”the king is said to be in check.
The game is won when one king is in check and cannot avoid capture on the next move - checkmate.

There are three possible results in chess: win, lose, or draw. There are three common ways a draw can come about:

  1. by mutual consent,
  2. when neither player has enough pieces to deliver checkmate,
  3. hen a player who is not in check has no legal move (stalemate)
